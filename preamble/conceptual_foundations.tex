

\section*{Core Constructs}

The trilogy operates on a set of shared constructs that describe internal representations and computational organization in neural architectures.

\textbf{Latent State.}
A representation $\mathbf{h}_t \in \latent$ encoding the internal state of the model at step $t$.

\textbf{Memory Field.}
A structured collection $\MemoryField$ of latent representations accessible through differentiable retrieval.

\textbf{Symbolic Representation.}
A compressed encoding $s \in \symspace$ derived from latent states.

\textbf{Routing Function.}
A mapping $\route{\cdot}{\expertspace}$ that governs allocation of computation across specialized modules.

\section*{Structural Relationship}

The trilogy examines the interaction between these constructs across three stages:
