# Canon of the Latent Cognition Research Program

## Canonical Hierarchy

### 1. Architectural Foundation

**Differentiable Latent Indexing in LLMs: Toward Native Retrieval as a Cognitive Function**

Role: Foundational architecture paper.

This paper defines the systems-level substrate of the research program. It establishes retrieval as a native differentiable function within transformer-based architectures rather than an external augmentation process.

Canonical status: Foundational.

Relationship to Trilogy: Architectural substrate.

---

### 2. Main Sequence

**Latent Cognition Trilogy**

Role: Main theoretical sequence.

The Trilogy develops the central conceptual, cognitive, and philosophical implications of latent cognition, bounded autonomy, and retrieval-native intelligence.

Canonical status: Main storyline.

Relationship to DLI-LLM: Builds on the architectural foundation but is not reducible to it.

---

### 3. Companion Hypotheses

The three hypothesis papers are formal satellite works. They preserve specific claims, constraints, or extensions that support the Trilogy without overloading the main manuscript.

Canonical status: Companion works.

Relationship to Trilogy: Supporting formalization.

---

### 4. Future Extensions

Future papers may extend the program into conscious AI, cybersecurity autonomy, health sciences, bounded decision systems, or enterprise AI governance.

Canonical status: Parallel expansions.

Relationship to Trilogy: Domain-specific or speculative extensions.
