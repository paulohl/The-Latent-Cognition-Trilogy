This repository contains foundational materials associated with the broader Latent Cognition Trilogy research program, a multi-stage investigation into retrieval-integrated transformer architectures, latent memory coordination, symbolic abstraction, and emergent cognitive organization in large language models.      

The present paper, Differentiable Latent Indexing in LLMs: Toward Native Retrieval as a Cognitive Function, establishes the architectural substrate upon which subsequent works in the research program are intended to build. The manuscript formalizes a retrieval-integrated transformer framework in which differentiable latent memory mechanisms participate directly within transformer-layer computation pathways.      

Unlike later works planned as part of the broader trilogy, this contribution is intentionally architecture-first, retrieval-focused, mathematically framed, systems-oriented, and methodologically restrained. It is designed as a standalone conceptual engineering paper suitable for independent peer review, citation, archival preservation, and future experimental extension.      

The broader Latent Cognition Trilogy extends beyond the architectural scope established here and will progressively explore topics such as persistent latent memory coordination, symbolic emergence, internal representation arbitration, retrieval-driven reasoning, bounded cognition, and layered memory architectures. These future investigations are expected to adopt a more integrative and interdisciplinary orientation while remaining grounded in formal architectural and computational modeling.      

A deliberate separation has therefore been established between the publishable architectural substrate represented by the present paper and the broader conceptual trajectory of the trilogy itself. This structure allows the DLI-LLM framework to serve as an independently citable scholarly artifact while also functioning as a foundational component of a larger long-term research program.      

Accordingly, future papers associated with the Latent Cognition Trilogy may reference and extend the architectural principles introduced in DLI-LLM, including differentiable latent retrieval, routing, and gating mechanisms, shared latent vector spaces, and retrieval-integrated transformer computation pathways.      

This repository preserves the evolving relationship between those standalone architectural contributions and the broader conceptual framework from which they emerge.
